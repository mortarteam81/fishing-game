## Visual North Star

Original warm hand-painted coastal adventure. The target mood is calm, premium, collectible, and gently cinematic: mineral blues, sea glass greens, coral accents, ochre sunlight, deep navy ink lines, and visible craft in silhouettes.

Avoid toddler proportions, toy-like plastic colors, neon gradients, and direct imitation of any named studio. Characters should read as young adult coastal explorers: smaller head ratio, longer legs, practical clothes, grounded stance, and deck-aware foot placement.

## Content Rules

- Sea friends: unique silhouette first, color second. Each biome family should have 2 common, 3 uncommon, 3 rare, and 2 special friends.
- Boats: stronger hull language with cabin, deck rail, wake, and tinted paint variations. Higher tiers add glow/wake effects without becoming sci-fi noise.
- Rods: material identity should be obvious from line color and icon tint. Higher tiers improve timing, lure speed, reel power, rarity, and mutation chance.
- Bait: small but readable food/object icons with distinct color families; higher tiers increase rare encounters and bite speed.
- Flags: readable heraldic motifs. Higher tiers can add subtle navigation, rare, or mutation bonuses.
- Fishing points: every route needs a different sky, water, landmark, and atmosphere so sailing feels like exploration rather than menu travel.
